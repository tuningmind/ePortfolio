Anti-Bullying Program

Writing prompt

"As a licensed Social Service Worker (having earned your BSW) you’ve been asked by your supervisor to develop a program to address the problem of bullying amongst the children and youth served in your agency."

"Although your programs vary in size and scope, the most promising announcements will incorporate many (but probably not all) of the following aspects: definition of bullying (both personal and cyber), prevalence of bullying, effects of bullying, warning signs of bullying, parental involvement if they suspect their child is being bullied, support tips for parents and school personnel, prevention, involvement of teachers and school administrators, what other students and youth can do to stop the bullying, effective interventions for stopping bullying."

"Utilizing the program plan complete each to help you conceptualize your program’s goal and objectives as well as identify the methods or interventions you’ve identified to reach your objectives."

"Have fun and be creative, artistic, innovative and resourceful in this project and above all please don’t take your ideas from an already developed program. Please develop your own program and implement your own ideas."

Program Plan

Problem #1:

Children who are bullied should not be expected to handle it alone. They are not in a conflict where both child and bully are partly right and partly wrong.

They are being victimized because there is a power imbalance between the child and the bully.

This is described in information about bullying provided by the United States government at stopbullying.gov , in the description “What Is Bullying” and their document “Misdirections in Bullying Prevention and Intervention” .

Goal #1:

When a child tells their parent they’ve been bullied at school, parents should be informed how to respond in a way that protects the child.

Objective 1.1: Engagingly list kinds of responses parents can give that will protect the child, so that parents have context for what they will say.

Objective 1.2: Illustrate sample responses parents can use to convey to the child how the child will be protected.

Intervention for Goal #1:

An infographic of parent responses to their child has been prepared, which can be printed and given to parents. The responses include sample wording parents can use as the basis for their own responses, in each of several categories designed to show the child that their parent is going to protect them.

Problem #2:

Parents don’t automatically know what to say to their children who say they’ve been bullied, as evidenced by my own experience as a parent. When I looked for help for what to say when my son told me he was being bullied at school, the information I had said to have the child work it out. When I reached out for more information online at the time, I found more of the same. Parents need to know about the information at stopbullying.gov and how they can use it.

Goal #2:

Help the parents be prepared to follow up on behalf of their child.

Make sure parents have the information they need to follow up on what they told the child they would do.

Internvention for Goal #2:

The infographic includes an area to write down names and phone numbers of school staff with roles in protecting children from bullying. These numbers should be made available to the parents at the time the infographic is given to them, and they should be guided and encouraged to ensure they write the numbers down so that if follow-up is needed, the contacts will be readily available.
